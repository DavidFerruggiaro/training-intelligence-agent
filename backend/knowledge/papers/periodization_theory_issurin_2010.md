# New Horizons for the Methodology and Physiology of Training Periodization

## Metadata
- **Authors**: Vladimir B. Issurin
- **Year**: 2010
- **Journal**: Sports Medicine
- **DOI**: 10.2165/11319770-000000000-00000

## Abstract

Training periodization represents the systematic planning of athletic preparation involving progressive cycling of training variables to optimize performance for targeted competitions. This review examines the evolution of periodization theory from the classical model proposed by Matveyev to contemporary block periodization approaches, evaluating the physiological rationale and practical application of each framework. The traditional periodization model, characterized by sequential phases of general preparation, specific preparation, and competition with gradually shifting emphasis from volume to intensity, has served as the foundation of training planning for decades. However, criticisms of this approach include its assumption of a single competitive peak, its difficulty accommodating the demands of modern sports with extended competitive calendars, and the potential interference effect when attempting to develop multiple fitness qualities simultaneously during mixed training phases. Block periodization addresses these limitations by concentrating training stimuli into focused mesocycle blocks of two to four weeks, each targeting a limited number of compatible fitness qualities. This concentrated loading approach is proposed to generate greater residual training effects through the accumulation of directed stimuli, while sequential ordering of blocks exploits the different decay rates of various fitness qualities to bring multiple capabilities to peak simultaneously. The accumulation block emphasizes basic abilities including aerobic capacity and muscular endurance, the transmutation block develops sport-specific qualities such as anaerobic power and maximal strength, and the realization block focuses on competitive readiness through reduced volume and event-specific preparation. Physiological evidence supports the notion that concentrated unidirectional training loads produce greater adaptive responses than mixed training approaches of equivalent total volume. However, the optimal sequencing, duration, and loading patterns within block periodization remain areas of active investigation, and the model may be most applicable to sports with clearly defined competitive periods rather than year-round competition schedules.

## Key Findings

- Block periodization concentrating training into focused two-to-four-week blocks targeting limited fitness qualities produces greater residual training effects than traditional mixed approaches
- The sequential ordering of accumulation, transmutation, and realization blocks exploits different fitness quality decay rates to achieve simultaneous peak performance
- Traditional periodization models with single annual peaks are poorly suited to modern sports with extended competitive calendars spanning many months
- Concentrated unidirectional training loads generate greater adaptive responses than equivalent total volume distributed across mixed training sessions
- The interference effect from simultaneous development of competing fitness qualities is a key physiological limitation of traditional multi-focus training phases

## Practical Implications

Coaches working with athletes in sports that have defined competitive seasons should consider implementing block periodization models with focused mesocycles of two to four weeks. Each block should prioritize a limited number of compatible training objectives rather than attempting to develop all fitness qualities simultaneously. The sequencing of blocks should account for the known residual training effects of different qualities: aerobic endurance has the longest residual effect, while speed and anaerobic power decay more rapidly and should be trained closer to competition. Athletes competing across extended seasons may benefit from repeating abbreviated block sequences within the competitive period to maintain multiple fitness qualities. Monitoring training response within each block is essential to confirm that the concentrated loading is producing the intended adaptive stimulus without excessive fatigue accumulation.
